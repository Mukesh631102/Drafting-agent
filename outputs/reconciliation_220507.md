#### 4. Procedures Performed  

1. Retrieved the approved Q1 fee schedule and confirmed the standard fee amount of $5,000.00.  
2. Searched the general ledger for duplicate entries of the same payer and amount; none were found.  
3. Inspected the transaction status field and traced the workflow to identify pending processing steps.  
4. Mapped the $5,000 receipt to the Q1 operating budget line‑item “Student Fees – Q1.”  
5. Reviewed internal policy documents governing fee recognition and revenue reporting.  

---

#### 5. Findings & Observations  

| Finding                     | Observation                                                                 |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Mathematical Consistency**| The $5,000 amount matches the approved Q1 fee schedule; no rounding, transcription, or duplicate issues were identified. |
| **Budget Discrepancies**    | The transaction remains “unprocessed,” creating a temporary shortfall in reported Q1 fee revenue. This variance will be eliminated once the entry is posted. |
| **Strategic Alignment**     | The fee directly supports the institution’s Q1 tuition‑revenue target and aligns with the strategic goal of maintaining stable enrollment‑related cash flows. |
| **Control Weakness**        | The existence of an unprocessed fee indicates a lapse in the posting workflow. No automated alert is currently triggered for entries pending beyond 24 hours. |

---

#### 6. Recommendations  

1. **Process Transaction** – Complete posting of the $5,000 entry and update the status to “Processed” without delay.  
2. **Attach Supporting Documentation** – Upload the corresponding invoice or receipt to the transaction record to satisfy audit evidence requirements.  
3. **Incorporate into Financial Statements** – Reflect the amount in the Q1 financial statements to correct the temporary revenue variance.  
4. **Enhance Monitoring Controls** – Implement an automated control that flags any fee entry remaining unprocessed for more than 24 hours, with escalation to the Finance Manager.  
5. **Periodic Review** – Conduct a quarterly review of unprocessed fee entries to ensure timely resolution and to mitigate recurrence of the identified control weakness.  

---

#### 7. Conclusion  

The transaction “ALICE J – Q1 FEES” is mathematically accurate, aligns with the institution’s revenue objectives, and complies with internal fee‑recognition policies. The only outstanding issue is its unprocessed status, which presently understates Q1 fee revenue. Prompt execution of the recommended actions will resolve the variance, reinforce internal controls, and ensure that financial reporting accurately reflects the institution’s fiscal position.
